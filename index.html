Woodworth Cabinetry Website Rebuild

Timeline: 2 Months

Tools: Cards, Sticky Notes, Adobe XD, Illustrator, Photoshop, Miro, Share folder, recorder

UX Techniques Used: User research, Interviews, Market research, Competitive/Comparative Analysis, Mind Mapping, Card Sorting, User Personas, Systems Map, Site Map, User Flows, Wireframing, Prototyping, Usability Testing, Visual design

Brief: Woodworth Cabinetry is a manufacturer offering kitchen and bath cabinetry to dealers. By 2021, the company decides to sell wood cabinetry for both end-users and dealers. The old website doesn‘t suit the need of presenting and selling goods to customers online and need a new functional and professional website.

Project goals:

  • Purchase: provide easy purchase processes for online individual users; add customer service and live help.
  • 3D Planning tool: allow users to plan and design their kitchen, and get a full 3D view of the new space
  • Easy Assemble: provide clear self-assemble instructions, for Covid safety concern and budget saving.
  • Simple Design: Design of website will represent simplicity and high quality of products.

Design Process

User Research

The user research start with a proto-persona and several user interviews target in property owners with age 24-60. The interviews focus on their online shopping habit on cabinetry and practice with Ikea kitchen planner. I used affinity diagram and empathy map to analysis users’ pain point. It results with the following user persona.

Market Research

To gain inspiration for the new website, I began by identifying 6 competitors, I start with analysis 3 famous online retail brand (indirect competitors) Ikea, Wayfair and Structure. Then dig deeper with 4 cabinet companies (direct competitors) Woodmark, Cabintes.com, Fabuwood and Kcdus, they all sells good in the market. My goal was to compare and identify common features across these sites and potential opportunities for Wood worth to differentiate itself.

Two key purpose from this activity was learning how different websites organized the category of cabinet and compare the process of 3D Home planner for each company. This was helpful information to design the processor punching and planning the 3D kitchen.

Information Architecture

After completing user and marketing research, I develop the navigation system by conducting a card sort, I organize all the tags into categories that seemed the most logical, then add titles that described each category. With the results of the card sort and inspiration from other competitor websites, I created a site map to define the overall site structure. I also create a system map to gain a better understanding of the functionality.

Wireframing and Prototyping

Once I got all my insights, I start with sketching the wireframe. By using the sit map as a guide, I sketch the wireframe on paper, then design with Adobe XD.

1, Home page

I kept the homepage a clean and simple design to avoid overwhelming users.

Hero space is the best selling series, it can easily changes to other content such as discount in the future. It follows by 3 main category.

The inspiration section allow users go to products links.

The bottom are easy accesses to 3 tool

2, Products pages

There are three path to narrow the right products. The users will going to choose a door style to start. Then select the type from that style, last choose for the function. After they find the right series, they can add item to cart or go inside the page of each item.

3, Kitchen Planner

3D Kitchen planner is a very important feature for cabinet website. It gives the users the ability to create a layout and take the guesswork out of how their finished kitchen will look.

  • Before the process start, a short guide video appears to help users understand how the tool works
  • Step 1, the users are able to choose a door style from 3 options.
  • BStep 2, choose the kitchen shape and size
  • Step 3, floor plan allow to add main kitchen elements and edit.
